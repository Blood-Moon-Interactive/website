Airlock — Pressurization & Water Lock System

Blood Moon InteractiveDoors

The Airlock behavior lets you build believable, mechanical pressure systems in GameGuru MAX. Whether you're creating a space station airlock, an underwater flood chamber, or a timed security gate, this behavior handles the door sequencing, pressure cycles, and environmental hazards behind the scenes.

Key Feature

Supports both air pressure cycling and water flood locking with automatic door sequencing, configurable timers, and optional NPC drowning mechanics.

Video Tutorial

Why Airlocks Matter

Airlocks are an excellent way to add depth and immersion to your levels. They can:

  • Create tension: Players must wait through timing cycles or manage resources while a door system cycles.
  • Enforce safety rules: Prevent players from entering hazardous environments without proper preparation.
  • Add environmental puzzles: Use pressure states and water levels to block or unlock paths.
  • Build atmosphere: A properly designed airlock heightens the feeling of realism in sci-fi and underwater settings.

Setup & Configuration

  1. Create two door objects in your scene to represent the inner and outer doors.
  2. Name them (default expected names are Inner Door and Outer Door).
  3. Create a switch object for the airlock and set AlwaysActive to ON.
  4. Attach the airlock behavior to the switch.
  5. Configure AIRLOCK_TYPE for an air or water lock.
  6. Set door names in DOOR1_NAME and DOOR2_NAME to match your objects.
  7. Adjust DELAY to control how long pressurization/depressurization takes.
  8. For water locks, configure WATER_LEVEL and enable NPC_DROWNING if desired.
  9. Add sound effects to Sound0 (activation) and Sound1 (processing) for a polished feel.
  10. Playtest the sequence to ensure doors and timers behave as expected.

Core Properties

  • AIRLOCK_TYPE: Choose between Air (pressure cycles) and Water (flooding lock).
  • AIR_LEVEL: Sets the air pressure level; players take damage if it drops during depressurization.
  • WATER_LEVEL: Controls the height of water in a water lock, determining how deep the chamber floods.
  • PROMPT_TEXT: The interaction prompt shown when the player is in range.
  • USE_RANGE: Maximum distance at which the player can trigger the airlock switch.
  • DEPRESSURISE_TEXT / PRESSURISE_TEXT: Countdown messages shown during the cycle.
  • DOOR1_NAME / DOOR2_NAME: The names of the two door entities controlled by the airlock.
  • DELAY: Time in seconds for pressurization/depressurization to complete.
  • NPC_DROWNING: Enables NPC drowning in water lock mode to increase the threat level.

Practical Implementation Examples

Space Station Airlock

Use the airlock to transition between safe interior rooms and the vacuum of space.

  • Set AIRLOCK_TYPE to Air and configure AIR_LEVEL to 100.
  • Set a long DELAY to make cycling feel deliberate.
  • Use PRESSURISE_TEXT and DEPRESSURISE_TEXT to communicate progress to the player.
  • Add sound effects for doors and cycling to reinforce the sci‑fi atmosphere.

Underwater Flood Chamber

Create a water‑filled airlock that floods and drains on a timer, forcing players to plan their movements.

  • Set AIRLOCK_TYPE to Water and adjust WATER_LEVEL to the desired flood height.
  • Enable NPC_DROWNING to add danger for enemies caught in the chamber.
  • Use doors and logic connections to create puzzles where cycling the airlock is required to reach new areas.

Advanced Techniques

  • Combine with logic triggers: Use GameGuru MAX logic connections to trigger environmental events when the airlock completes its cycle.
  • Create timed challenges: Force players to move quickly by adding hazards that activate when the airlock starts cycling.
  • Use shared door names: Control multiple airlocks by reusing door names across objects for synchronized sequences.
  • Mix air and water modes: Use two airlocks in sequence—one air and one water—for a multi‑stage puzzle or escape sequence.

Common Pitfalls & Solutions

Problem Solution
Doors never open Verify DOOR1_NAME and DOOR2_NAME exactly match the entity names in your scene.
Airlock doesn't cycle Make sure AlwaysActive is ON and that the switch object is not disabled by other logic.
Water lock doesn’t flood Check WATER_LEVEL and ensure AIRLOCK_TYPE is set to Water.
Player takes damage unexpectedly For air locks, adjust AIR_LEVEL or disable player damage behavior if you want a non‑lethal sequence.

Design Best Practices

Make Timing Feel Natural

  • Set DELAY so players have time to react but still feel urgency.
  • Use audio cues to signal when a cycle starts and ends.

Use Visual Feedback

  • Label your doors clearly (e.g., Inner and Outer) so players understand which door is safe to pass through.
  • Use lighting or particle effects to show when the chamber is pressurized or flooded.

Related Behaviors

Conclusion

The Airlock behavior is a powerful tool for adding mechanical depth and environmental storytelling to your levels. Whether you’re building a sci‑fi station, an underwater base, or a puzzle room, airlocks provide a consistent, reliable system that players can interact with and rely on.

Start by building a basic two‑door airlock, then experiment with pressure, water, and logic triggers to create more advanced sequences and puzzles.
